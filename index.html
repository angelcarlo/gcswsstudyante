GUIDANCE AND ADMISSION

PHILOSOPHY

The Guidance Program of Gordon College is based on the philosophical and psychological assumptions, which those individuals are positive, forward, moving basically good, rational, socialized, realistic, cooperative, constructive and trustworthy. They are ultimately self-actualizing. They know what is best for themselves under normal or ideal conditions. However, when problems and difficulties arise, the individual is lead to believe that he is unworthy which often lead to actions that may harm themselves and others. In this case, the individual must be helped to become a fully functioning person with optimal psychological adjustment and maturity. In other words, Gordon College students must be helped to move towards self-actualization or experience full humanness in through enjoyment of life in all this aspects.

VISION

The Guidance and Counseling Office shares and supports the vision-mission of Gordon College committed to the holistic development of students and society. The office envisions the Guidance Program to be an effective instrument in assisting students realized their full potentials and become effective members of society.

MISSION

The Guidance and Counseling Office provides assistance and service to students’ developmental needs and help them become dynamic, competitive individuals imbued with values and guide them to be secured, forward-moving, rational, self-directed and fully functioning member of society.

SCHOLARSHIP UNIT AND STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS

STUDENT DISCIPLINE

Student Discipline refers to the judicious implementation o institutional rules and regulations governing student behavior and conduct.

The HEI shall have gender and disability sensitive rules and regulations formulated in consultation with students and faculty and published in a student manual that is accessible and disseminated to students including students with disabilities, faculty and concurred in by parents. The rules and regulations for misconduct such as but not limited to acts vandalism, exaggerated utterances, irresponsible and libelous statements and other negative acts of militancy that threaten peace and order and private and public properties inside and outside the HEIs.

A discipline committee shall be established in all HEIs to ensure due process in dealing with student misconduct.

STUDENT LINGKAGES AND REPLACEMENT

Higher education Institution shall institute valid appraisal data of students for career and job placement. They shall have continuous follow-up and monitoring of student placement conducted on regular basis.

Higher education Institution shall maintain active networking with school, community, alumni and other relevant agencies for career and job placement of students.

Informative materials in accessible formats on career and job opportunities shall be provided and skills development programs shall be made available.

There shall be regular career seminars and job placement services available for the students.

There shall be mechanisms to institutionalize the link with industries.

The students must be informed of the timelines for the concerned HEI’s assistance in seeking career and job placement at least until a specified period of time.

STUDENT PUBLICATION UNIT

GENERAL PRINCIPLES AND OBJECTIVES

The Forefront lives with its tagline, "Informing and Empowering." To be able to uphold with its battlecry for campus press freedom, the Editorial Board sets the following General Principles:

The Forefront shall serve primarily as a newspaper of and for the students. It shall protect and promote the constitutionally mandated rights of students to free speech and of expression. In addition, it shall protect and promote the students' rights and welfare.

The Forefront, being a publication of the students devoted to the ideals of truth, shall pursue the mission of social transformation through active and responsible journalism.

The Forefront, being an independent institution, shall not be subjected to any form of censorship. In turn, the editors and staff shall see to it that the Forefront exercises academic and campus press freedom in accordance with the laws of the Republic of the Philippines. It shall also seek to protect and promote press freedom by taking firm and assertive stance against elements endangering such right.

The editors shall respect the rights of other parties that are equally protected by the laws of the Republic of the Philippines, affording them the right and opportunity to refute or reply to criticism or comment hurled at them.

STUDENT MANUAL

Students, or GC Students, as referred to in this handbook, are those who are officially enrolled in any Graduate, Baccalaureate or Vocational courses which are regularly offered by the College. Those enrolled in special crash courses shall be covered by this Manual, except in the wearing of school uniform.

Students shall be expected to act as responsible citizens who show respect to proper authority and fellow students while they are in campus.

Students must familiarize themselves with the contents of this handbook, and strictly comply with all the provisions stated herein.

School administrators and faculty members shall exercise authority over students, and shall act as the students' parents who must be responsible for correcting misbehaviors in and off campus. All school personnel, even fellow students, possess the right and duty to report to the Office of Student Welfare and Services violations of the Gordon College rules and regulations committed in their presence.

SURVIVAL KIT

AT HOME:

Turn on your study mode! Devote a regular 2-3 hours a day to study in your zone.

Select a well-lighted location with proper ventilation and lighting. Ensure that it will be far from distractions. This place should fit your specific personality and learning style.

Devote this place for studying and accomplishing school work

Place your books, pens, papers, notebooks, laptops and other materials strategically located at arms-length so you have quick access to them.

Drink water or juice to keep your brain active.

Hold back the desire to do text messaging, play your Sony PlayStations, Xbox, internet surfing and computer games. Know that this time is a priority investment for your future.

Take 3-5 minute breaks every hour of designated study time to keep our mind fresh and relaxed.

AT SCHOOL:

Find a study group or a study buddy who share the same aspirations as you do- eager to study the subjects and aims for the best marks in class.

Places to really study would be the School Library, the Public Library, or the Student Lounge. Keep your discussions at a minimum as other students are studying too.
